Album

Cites With Iconic Towers

Explore four incredible cities known for their iconic towers through this album. From soaring skyscrapers to historic landmarks, each track pays tribute to the unique architectural wonders that grace these global destinations.

Tokyo, Japan
The Tokyo Skytree was designed by the architects from Nikken Sekkei, the process of building the tower began in 2008 and was completed on February 2012. It is found that, with more than half a century of existence, the Tokyo Tower represents the successful post-war Japanese society, while in just five years the Tokyo Skytree has become a symbol of Japanese national spirit and resilience in an era of multiple crises.

Paris, France
The Eiffel Tower was built to be one the main attractions at the Paris World's Fair in 1889. That year, the World's Fair covered the entire Champ de Mars in Paris and its focus was the vast constructions in iron and steel that were the great industrial advancement of that time.

Pisa, Italy
The construction of the Leaning Tower of Pisa began in August 1173. It was interrupted several times by wars, debt and while engineers worked on solutions to correct the lean. We now know that without these interruptions that allowed the soil to compress under the tower, it would have certainly toppled over.

New York, NY, USA
The Empire State Building is a 102-story Art Deco skyscraper in Midtown Manhattan, New York City.
Intended to be the world's first 100+ story building, construction of the Empire State Building began on March 17, 1930. Construction was completed in a record-breaking 1 year and 45 days. Beautiful inside and out, the Empire State Building is an architectural marvel beloved across the world.
